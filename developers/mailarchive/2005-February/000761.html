Hi,

I started looking at CLI-UNO and how to make it usable on Linux with
Mono. Yesterday I read lots of English, today C++ and C#:

The "English" was:

http://api.openoffice.org/docs/DevelopersGuide/ProfUNO/ProfUNO.html to
refresh my UNO

http://udk.openoffice.org/common/man/bridge.html to understand what a
"Bridge" is

http://api.openoffice.org/docs/DevelopersGuide/AdvancedUNO/AdvancedUNO.html
and http://udk.openoffice.org/cpp/man/cpp_bridges.html with an annotated
and simplified C++-Bridge

The C++ and C# is here
http://go-oo.org/bonsai/rview.cgi?cvsroot=/var/cvsup&dir=udk/cli_ure&module=OpenOffice
(read most of this) and from here
http://go-oo.org/bonsai/rview.cgi?cvsroot=/var/cvsup&dir=udk/bridges/source/cli_uno&module=OpenOffice
(read only cli_bridge.cxx so far)

Along the way, I discovered that core UNO has a C interface, but the OOo
developers generally prefer C++ and many useful helper functions are
available only from C++. Added more notes to mono.txt
http://cvs.gnome.org/viewcvs/ooo-build/doc/mono.txt?view=markup and
tried to find the places where managed-unmanaged transitions take place
in the C++ code.

In the background I tried to build src680-m76 to have a hacking tree. No
luck yet.
